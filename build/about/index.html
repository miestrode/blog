Hello, this is my blog.

When most people want to put their thoughts out there, they probably don't think of creating a blog. When they do, they usually don't host it on a personal website. When they do, they usually don't use an SSG as unpopular as Zola. That doesn't matter however, as I am neither most, nor people. So we go on.

This blog was forged near the end of 2022, after I've decided it would a good idea to have a place to put all my ideas in (and also because I despartely wanted an excuse to get a VPS). It is no ordinary blog, unless you're a programmer, in which case this blog is quite ordinary. It's a blog of Romance, Sarcasm, Math, and Language. Just kidding! Although two of these terms are correct, but you won't know which. This is also a blog about programming and mistakes, a lot of mistakes.

Who are you?

My name is Yoav Grimland, and I am a student and amateur programmer, programming, solving problems, playing, learning and cooking in his free time. When I program, I mainly work on things related to compilers and language design, although I like to think my programming interests are expanding. I am currently learning Haskell, a pure functional programming language, and am interested in learning more.

You can find out more about my work on my Github.

Why is the blog named "Looking both ways"?

Because good programmers always looks both ways before crossing a one-way street.
